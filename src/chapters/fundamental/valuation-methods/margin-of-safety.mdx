# Margin of Safety

Margin of safety is the buffer between the price you pay and the intrinsic value. It's your protection against errors and uncertainty.

<InfoBox type="note">
  "The function of the margin of safety is, in essence, that of rendering unnecessary an accurate estimate of the future." – Benjamin Graham
</InfoBox>

## What is Margin of Safety?

The difference between what something is worth and what you pay:

**Margin of Safety = (Intrinsic Value - Price) / Intrinsic Value × 100**

Example:
- Intrinsic Value: ₹100
- Current Price: ₹70
- Margin of Safety: (100 - 70) / 100 = 30%

Depends on uncertainty:

/>

<InfoBox type="tip">
  When uncertain, demand more margin. It's better to miss an opportunity than to lose money.
</InfoBox>

## Calculating Margin of Safety

### Method 1: Based on Intrinsic Value

1. Calculate intrinsic value (DCF or multiple-based)
2. Compare to current price
3. Calculate percentage difference

### Method 2: Based on Downside Scenario

1. Estimate worst-case value
2. Ensure price is near or below this
3. Protects against adverse scenarios

### Method 3: Based on Historical Valuation

1. Find lowest historical P/E
2. Apply to current earnings
3. Compare to current price

## When to Compromise on Margin

Rarely, but consider:
- Exceptional quality with clear growth
- You're averaging into a position
- Long-term holding horizon
- Very high conviction

But even then, don't abandon margin completely.

## Margin of Safety Checklist

Before buying, confirm:
- [ ] I've estimated intrinsic value
- [ ] Price offers adequate margin
- [ ] Margin is appropriate for risk level
- [ ] I can be wrong and still not lose much
- [ ] I'm not buying due to FOMO

## Key Takeaways

- Margin of safety is the buffer between price and value
- Required margin depends on uncertainty
- More risk = more margin needed
- Don't abandon margin even for quality companies
- It's your protection against being wrong
