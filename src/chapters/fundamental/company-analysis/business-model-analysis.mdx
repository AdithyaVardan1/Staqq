# Business Model Analysis

Understanding how a company makes money is the foundation of fundamental analysis. Every investment decision starts with the business model.

<InfoBox type="note">
  A business model describes how a company creates value, delivers it to customers, and captures a portion as profit.
</InfoBox>

## What is a Business Model?

The business model answers:
- What does the company sell?
- Who buys it?
- How do they reach customers?
- What makes them money?
- Why can they keep making money?

## Cost Structure

### Fixed Costs

Stay same regardless of volume:
- Rent, salaries, insurance
- High fixed costs = Operating leverage
- Good when volume grows, risky when it falls

### Variable Costs

Change with volume:
- Raw materials, commissions
- Lower risk but less leverage
- Margins more stable but limited upside

<InfoBox type="important">
  Companies with high fixed costs magnify both gains and losses. Understand leverage before investing.
</InfoBox>

## Comparing Business Models

Within same industry, compare:
- Which model generates higher margins?
- Which requires less capital?
- Which has more recurring revenue?
- Which scales better?

## Key Takeaways

- Business model explains how money is made
- Revenue model affects predictability
- Cost structure affects risk profile
- Customer mix determines concentration risk
- Better margins usually mean stronger business
