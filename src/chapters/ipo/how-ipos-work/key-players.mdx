# Key Players

Every IPO involves multiple parties who play crucial roles. Understanding who they are helps you interpret the offering better.

<InfoBox type="note">
  The quality of participants – especially lead managers and the company's management – often indicates the quality of the IPO.
</InfoBox>

## The Company (Issuer)

The company going public:
- Defines the offering structure
- Prepares disclosure documents
- Uses proceeds as described in prospectus
- Liable for accurate disclosures

## Promoters

Founders and controlling shareholders:
- Often retain significant stake post-IPO
- May sell part of holding (OFS)
- Lock-in periods apply (typically 3 years for promoters)

Watch for:
- Promoter track record
- Post-IPO shareholding
- Pledged shares

<InfoBox type="tip">
  High promoter stake post-IPO (65%+) shows confidence. Large OFS by promoters might signal they want out.
</InfoBox>

## Underwriters

Guarantee that shares will be subscribed:
- If issue undersubscribed, underwriter buys remaining
- Reduces risk for the company
- Most IPOs are fully underwritten

<InfoBox type="important">
  Underwriting doesn't guarantee the price will hold post-listing. It only ensures the IPO subscription succeeds.
</InfoBox>

## Registrar to the Issue

Handles logistics:
- Collects applications
- Manages allotment process
- Coordinates refunds
- Credits shares to Demat accounts

Major registrars:
- Link Intime
- KFin Technologies
- Bigshare Services

## Regulator: SEBI

Securities and Exchange Board of India:
- Reviews and approves prospectus
- Ensures adequate disclosures
- Protects investor interests
- Can reject or ask for changes

SEBI approval is mandatory but doesn't endorse the IPO quality.

## Stock Exchanges

NSE and BSE:
- Approve listing
- Provide trading platform
- Set listing day mechanics
- Monitor trading activity

## Credit Rating Agencies

For IPOs involving debt:
- Rate the company's creditworthiness
- CRISIL, ICRA, CARE, India Ratings

- Identify risk factors
- Ensure regulatory compliance

## Key Takeaways

- Multiple parties work together for an IPO
- Lead manager quality matters for execution
- Promoter track record and post-IPO stake are crucial
- SEBI approval doesn't mean buy recommendation
- Quality of participants indicates quality of issue
