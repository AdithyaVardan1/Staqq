# Application Process

Applying for an IPO is straightforward once you understand the process. This chapter walks you through the steps.

<InfoBox type="note">
  You need a Demat account and trading account with a SEBI-registered broker to apply for IPOs. The process is fully online.
</InfoBox>

## Application Methods

### 1. Through Broker (Recommended)

Most brokers offer IPO application:
- Zerodha, Groww, Upstox, Angel One, etc.
- Simple interface in the app or website
- Track status easily

### 2. Through UPI Apps

Some banks and payment apps:
- BHIM app
- Google Pay (limited)
- Bank's own UPI app

### 3. Through NetBanking

ASBA facility through bank:
- Login to netbanking
- Find IPO section
- Apply directly

<InfoBox type="tip">
  Broker platforms are usually the easiest. The interface is designed for investors, and they guide you through the process.
</InfoBox>

## ASBA: Application Supported by Blocked Amount

How ASBA works:
1. You apply for IPO
2. Amount is blocked in your bank account
3. Money stays with you (earning interest)
4. If allotted, amount is debited
5. If not allotted, block is released

## Multiple Applications

### What's Allowed

- One application per PAN per IPO (retail category)
- Family members can apply separately
- Different Demat accounts with different PANs

### What's NOT Allowed

- Multiple applications from same PAN
- Will be rejected or clubbed
- Can lead to rejection of all applications

<InfoBox type="warning">
  Applying multiple times with the same PAN will get all your applications rejected. One application only.
</InfoBox>

## Key Takeaways

- Apply through your broker for easiest experience
- Choose cut-off price for best allotment chances
- Authorize UPI mandate promptly
- Only one application per PAN allowed
- ASBA keeps your funds in bank until allotment
