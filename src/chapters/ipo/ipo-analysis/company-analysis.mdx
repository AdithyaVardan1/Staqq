# Company Analysis

Before investing in any IPO, you need to understand the business thoroughly. This chapter outlines a framework for analyzing IPO companies.

<InfoBox type="note">
  The best IPO investments are in great businesses at fair prices. Understanding the company is step one.
</InfoBox>

## Quality Indicators

### Consistent Performance

- Revenue growth over 3-5 years
- Margin stability or improvement
- Return ratios (ROE, ROCE) above cost of capital

### Cash Generation

- Operating cash flow positive
- Free cash flow generation
- Low working capital intensity

### Clean Balance Sheet

- Low debt levels
- Manageable related party exposure
- Conservative accounting

## Key Takeaways

- Understand how the company makes money
- Look for sustainable competitive advantages
- Assess management quality and alignment
- Identify growth drivers for the future
- Watch for red flags that signal problems
