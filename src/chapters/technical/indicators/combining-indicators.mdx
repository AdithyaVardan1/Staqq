# Combining Indicators

One indicator isn't enough. But too many create confusion. Here's how to combine indicators effectively.

<InfoBox type="note">
  The goal of combining indicators is confirmation – multiple signals pointing the same way. More isn't always better.
</InfoBox>

## Why Combine?

Single indicators:
- Give false signals
- Work better in some conditions than others
- Can be improved with confirmation

Multiple indicators:
- Filter out false signals
- Confirm trend and momentum
- Increase probability of success

Don't stack indicators from the same category (e.g., RSI + Stochastic = redundant).

## Effective Combinations

### Combination 1: MA + RSI

- Moving Average: Trend direction
- RSI: Entry timing

Signal: Buy when price pulls back to rising MA with RSI at 30-40.

### Combination 2: MACD + Support/Resistance

- Support/Resistance: Key levels
- MACD: Momentum confirmation

Signal: Buy at support when MACD gives bullish crossover.

### Combination 3: Multiple Timeframes

- Higher timeframe: Trend direction
- Lower timeframe: Entry timing

Signal: Daily trend up, 4-hour MACD crossover for entry.

<InfoBox type="tip">
  Start with one trend indicator and one momentum indicator. Add more only if you understand them well.
</InfoBox>

## Common Mistake: Curve Fitting

"I'll add this indicator because it would have caught that move."

Problems:
- Works in hindsight, fails in real-time
- Creates an over-optimized system
- Adds complexity without improving results

<InfoBox type="important">
  Keep it simple. The best traders use 2-4 indicators they understand deeply, not a dozen they barely know.
</InfoBox>

## Practical Framework

1. **Primary indicator:** Identifies trade direction
2. **Confirmation indicator:** Confirms entry timing
3. **Filter:** Keeps you out of bad trades

Example:
- Primary: Price above 50 EMA (trend up)
- Confirmation: RSI bounce from 40 (momentum support)
- Filter: Only trade if volume is above average

## Key Takeaways

- Combine indicators from different categories
- 3-4 indicators is typically optimal
- More indicators doesn't mean better analysis
- Wait for alignment before trading
- Keep it simple and understand what you use
