# Continuation Patterns

Continuation patterns suggest that the market is just taking a breather. The trend is likely to continue in the same direction after the pattern completes.

## Triangles

Triangles represent a pause in the trend with converging price action (volatility contraction).

### Ascending Triangle (Bullish)
- **Top:** Flat resistance line.
- **Bottom:** Rising support line (higher lows).
- **Psychology:** Buyers are getting aggressive (paying higher prices), sellers are holding a fixed level. Eventually, sellers run out and price pops up.

### Descending Triangle (Bearish)
- **Bottom:** Flat support line.
- **Top:** Falling resistance line (lower highs).
- **Psychology:** Sellers are getting aggressive, buyers holding a level. Eventually, buyers are exhausted.

### Symmetrical Triangle
- Both lines converging.
- Can break either way, but usually follows the prior trend.

## Flags and Pennants

These are short-term patterns that look like a flag on a pole.
- **The Pole:** Sharp, strong move (high volume).
- **The Flag:** Small consolidation channel against the trend (low volume).
- **The Break:** Continuation of the original move (volume returns).

<InfoBox type="tip">
  "Flags fly half-mast." The target for a flag pattern is often the length of the pole, added to the breakout point.
</InfoBox>

## Cup and Handle

A long-term bullish continuation pattern.
1. **Cup:** "U" shaped consolidation.
2. **Handle:** Small pullback near the top (like a flag).
3. **Breakout:** Price clears the rim of the cup.
