# Candlestick Patterns

Candlestick patterns are the "short-term" signals of technical analysis. They often signal reversals or continuations over 1-5 days.

## Bullish Patterns (Buy Signals)

### Hammer
- Small body at top, long lower wick (2x body).
- **Context:** At support after a downtrend.
- **Meaning:** Sellers pushed low, buyers rejected it.

### Bullish Engulfing
- Red candle followed by a bigger Green candle that technically "engulfs" the previous body.
- **Context:** Bottom of a downtrend.
- **Meaning:** Buyers have overwhelmed sellers.

### Morning Star
- 3 Candles: Big Red -> Small Body (Star) -> Big Green.
- **Meaning:** Momentum shift from down to up.

## Bearish Patterns (Sell Signals)

### Shooting Star
- Inverted Hammer shape at the *top* of a trend.
- Long upper wick.
- **Meaning:** Buyers tried to push up, sellers slammed it down.

### Bearish Engulfing
- Green candle followed by a bigger Red candle.
- **Context:** Top of an uptrend.

### Evening Star
- 3 Candles: Big Green -> Small Body (Star) -> Big Red.
