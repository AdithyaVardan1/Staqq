# Reversal Chart Patterns

Reversal patterns signal that the current trend is running out of steam and a new trend in the opposite direction is likely to begin. Unlike candlestick reversals which happen over 1-3 days, these chart patterns take weeks or months to form.

## Head and Shoulders

The Head and Shoulders (H&S) is the most famous reversal pattern. It signals the end of an uptrend.

### Structure
1. **Left Shoulder:** Price rises to a peak and declines.
2. **Head:** Price rises above the previous peak and declines.
3. **Right Shoulder:** Price rises again but fails to reach the Head's height, then declines.
4. **Neckline:** The support line connecting the lows.

**Trade Signal:** Short when price breaks below the Neckline.
**Target:** The vertical distance from Head to Neckline, projected downward.

### Inverse Head and Shoulders
The bullish version at the bottom of a downtrend.
- Buyers push price up (Left Shoulder)
- Sellers push it lower (Head)
- Buyers push it up again (Right Shoulder)
- Break of Neckline = Long Signal.

## Double Tops and Bottoms

### Double Top (The "M" Pattern)
Likely top of an uptrend.
- Price hits a resistance level (Peak 1).
- Pulls back.
- Hits the same resistance level again (Peak 2) and fails to break it.
- **Signal:** Sell when price breaks below the "Valley" (support) between the two peaks.

### Double Bottom (The "W" Pattern)
Likely bottom of a downtrend.
- Price hits support (Low 1).
- Bounces.
- Hits support again (Low 2).
- **Signal:** Buy when price breaks above the peak between the two lows.

## Triple Tops and Bottoms
Similar to Double Tops/Bottoms but with three touches. They are rarer but considered stronger signals because the level has been tested three times and held.

<InfoBox type="important">
  Volume is key for reversals. For valid breakouts/breakdowns, volume should increase significantly to confirm the move. A breakout on low volume is often a trap.
</InfoBox>
