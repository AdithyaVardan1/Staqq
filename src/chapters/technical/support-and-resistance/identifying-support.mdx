# Identifying Support

Support is a price level where buying interest is strong enough to overcome selling pressure. Price tends to bounce off support.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Support acts like a floor – it's where buyers step in, preventing further decline. Understanding support levels helps you find better entry points.
</InfoBox>

## What is Support?

Support occurs where:
- Buyers are willing to buy
- Selling pressure is absorbed
- Price tends to bounce higher

It's a psychological level where traders remember "this is where it bounced before."

## How to Identify Support

### Previous Lows

The most basic support – where price has bottomed before.

<Table 
  headers={["Look For","Why It Matters"]}
  rows={[
    ["Multiple touches", "More tests = stronger level"]
  ]}
/>
<InfoBox type="tip">
  Support that has been tested multiple times becomes stronger. Each test reinforces the level in traders' minds.
</InfoBox>

### Round Numbers

Psychological levels where orders cluster:
- ₹100, ₹500, ₹1000, ₹10000
- Human preference for round numbers

### Moving Averages

Price often bounces off moving averages:
- 20-day EMA (short-term)
- 50-day EMA (medium-term)
- 200-day MA (long-term, major support)

### Gap Fills

When price gaps down, the gap area often acts as future support.

### Trendlines

Upward sloping lines connecting higher lows can act as dynamic support.

## Support Becomes Resistance

When support breaks:
- Old support becomes new resistance
- This is called "polarity change"
- Price often returns to test from below

Example:
- Support at ₹100 breaks
- Price falls to ₹90
- Rallies back toward ₹100
- ₹100 now acts as resistance (ceiling)

## Practical Application

### For buyers:
- Look to buy near support levels
- Set stop loss below support
- Risk is limited, reward potentially unlimited

### For holders:
- Support break is a warning signal
- Consider reducing position or exiting

## Key Takeaways

- Support is where buying overcomes selling
- Previous lows and round numbers are common support levels
- Think in zones, not exact prices
- Multiple tests strengthen but eventually weaken support
- Broken support becomes resistance
