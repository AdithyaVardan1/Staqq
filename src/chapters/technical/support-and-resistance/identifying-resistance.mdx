# Identifying Resistance

Resistance is a price level where selling pressure is strong enough to overcome buying. Price tends to reverse down from resistance.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Resistance acts like a ceiling – it's where sellers step in, preventing further advance. Understanding resistance helps you time exits and set targets.
</InfoBox>

## What is Resistance?

Resistance occurs where:
- Sellers are willing to sell
- Buying pressure is absorbed
- Price tends to reverse lower

It's where traders remember "this is where it stopped going up before."

## How to Identify Resistance

### Previous Highs

The most basic resistance – where price has peaked before.

### Round Numbers

Same as support – orders cluster at:
- ₹100, ₹500, ₹1000, ₹10000
- Often combined with previous highs

<InfoBox type="tip">
  A stock approaching ₹1000 for the first time often faces resistance. Traders take profits, new buyers hesitate.
</InfoBox>

### Moving Averages

Price often stalls at moving averages in downtrends:
- 20-day EMA
- 50-day EMA
- 200-day MA (major resistance in downtrends)

### Gap Areas

When price gaps up, the gap area often acts as future resistance if price revisits.

### Trendlines

Downward sloping lines connecting lower highs act as dynamic resistance.

## Resistance Becomes Support

When resistance breaks:
- Old resistance becomes new support
- Price often pulls back to test it
- Successful test confirms breakout

Example:
- Resistance at ₹100 breaks
- Price rises to ₹115
- Pulls back to ₹100
- ₹100 now acts as support (floor)

## All-Time Highs

When a stock breaks all-time highs:
- No overhead resistance
- Buyers are all in profit
- Can run free

<InfoBox type="tip">
  Stocks at all-time highs can keep rising. There's no overhead supply from trapped buyers waiting to exit.
</InfoBox>

## Practical Application

### For buyers:
- Be cautious buying at resistance
- Wait for breakout confirmation
- Or buy pullback after successful breakout

### For holders:
- Consider taking profits at resistance
- Or use trailing stops above resistance

### For sellers:
- Resistance is where shorts are initiated
- Stop loss above resistance zone

## Key Takeaways

- Resistance is where selling overcomes buying
- Previous highs and round numbers are common resistance levels
- Think in zones, not exact prices
- Broken resistance becomes support
- All-time highs have no overhead resistance
