# Breakouts

A breakout occurs when price moves decisively beyond a support or resistance level. Breakouts can signal the start of powerful new trends.

<InfoBox type="note">
  Breakouts represent a shift in supply and demand. Buyers overpower sellers (resistance break) or sellers overpower buyers (support break).
</InfoBox>

## Types of Breakouts

### Resistance Breakout (Bullish)

Price moves above a resistance level:
- Buyers overcome sellers
- New uptrend may begin
- Typically accompanied by volume surge

### Support Breakdown (Bearish)

Price moves below a support level:
- Sellers overcome buyers
- New downtrend may begin
- Often accelerates on fear

</InfoBox>

## The Breakout-Retest Pattern

A common and reliable pattern:

1. Price breaks out above resistance
2. Continues higher briefly
3. Pulls back to old resistance
4. Old resistance acts as new support
5. Price bounces and continues higher

Why this works:
- Gives latecomers a chance to buy
- Confirms new support level
- Shakes out weak hands

<InfoBox type="tip">
  The retest (pullback) often offers a lower-risk entry than buying the initial breakout.
</InfoBox>

### Stop Loss Placement

For resistance breakouts:
- Below the breakout level
- Below the retest low
- Allow for some noise

### Target Setting

- Previous resistance levels above
- Measured moves (pattern height projected)
- Trailing stops for trend following

## Breakout Failures

Even valid breakouts can fail. When they do:

- Treat it as new information
- Cut losses quickly
- Failed breakouts can become powerful reversals

<InfoBox type="warning">
  A failed breakout to the upside often leads to aggressive selling. Trapped buyers exit, adding to selling pressure.
</InfoBox>
