# Risk Reward Ratio

The Holy Grail of trading isn't a magical indicator; it's proper risk management. Specifically, the Risk:Reward (R:R) ratio.

## What is Risk:Reward?

It is the relationship between potential loss and potential gain on a trade.

- **Risk:** The amount you lose if your stop loss is hit.
- **Reward:** The amount you gain if your target is hit.

**Formula:**
`R:R = (Target Price - Entry Price) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)`

## Why is it Critical?

If you have a **1:2 R:R Ratio** (risking $1 to make $2), you only need to be right **34% of the time** to break even.

## Position Sizing

Knowing your R:R is step one. Step two is sizing your bet.
**The Golden Rule:** Never risk more than 1-2% of your total trading capital on a single trade.

### Calculation
`Shares to Buy = (Total Capital * Risk %) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)`

**Example:**
- Capital: $10,000
- Risk per trade: 1% ($100)
- Entry: $50
- Stop Loss: $48 (Risk per share = $2)
- Shares to Buy = $100 / $2 = **50 shares**

Even if you seek a 1000% return, if you bet 100% of your account and lose, you are out of the game.
